We've been building things on Atlassian's Forge platform for a while now — apps that ship to the Marketplace and bespoke integrations for customers who need something more than a generic plugin can give them. Along the way we've collected a lot of small lessons about Forge, about Cloud-first development, and about what makes an Atlassian app actually pleasant to use.

This blog is where we'll share those lessons.

## What you can expect

A mix of:

- **Forge tales from the trenches** — the gotchas, the workarounds, and the patterns that have held up across multiple apps.
- **Marketplace go-to-market notes** — listing pages, pricing models, customer support, what moves the needle.
- **Customer story write-ups** — anonymized when needed, concrete enough to be useful.
- **Opinions** — we'll have a few. The Atlassian ecosystem deserves better tools, and we're not shy about saying which ones we'd build.

## Why now

Mostly because the platform changes fast. Forge is moving quickly, the migration from Connect-on-Cloud is finally winding down, and Cloud-only customers have a different shape of need than the ones we used to serve on Server. We learn from writing as much as you might from reading — so this blog is a forcing function for us, too.

Subscribe via [RSS](/rss.xml) if that's your thing. Otherwise, you'll find us linking to new posts on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/numericoasis).

— The Numeric Oasis team
